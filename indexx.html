my favorite poem

the plowman and his children

Work, take the trouble:
This is the fund that is lacking the least.
A rich Plowman, sensing his imminent death,
Bringing his children, spoke to them without witnesses.
Beware, he said, to sell the inheritance that our parents left us.
A treasure is hidden inside.
I don't know the place; but a little courage
You will find it, you will overcome it.
Stir your field as soon as you have made the Outage.
Dig, excavate, dig; do not leave any place
Where the hand does not pass and repass.
The father dead, the sons return the field to you
Here, beyond, everywhere;
so that at the end of the year He brought in more. To show them before his death
That work is a treasure.
Jean de La Fontaine
